How to plan a successful streaming service: A comprehensive guide (and why Laminar is the right partner)
Scroll to navigate
By Narendra Nag
With the right content, user experience, marketing, and monetization strategies, your streaming service has the potential to thrive in this booming market.

The streaming media industry is experiencing a meteoric rise. In 2022, the global streaming media market is expected to be worth a staggering $157.4 billion. The increasing popularity of streaming services, such as Netflix, Hulu, and Amazon Prime Video globally, and strong regional players, is being driven by the growing number of users who (have cancelled their cable connections and) are willing to pay for content delivered to them over the internet.

If you're a media company, you may be considering launching your own streaming service to tap into this lucrative market. Before you jump in, there are several factors you need to consider to maximise your chances of success.

Define your target audience and niche

The first step in launching a streaming service is to define your target audience. Determine who you are trying to reach with your service and what kind of content they're interested in. Are you targeting a specific age group, region, or interest? Once you know your target audience, you can start developing a content strategy that will appeal to them. Furthermore, finding a niche market to differentiate yourself from competitors can help you stand out in a crowded market.

Secure the rights to high-quality content and create original programming

The most crucial factor in the success of a streaming service is the quality of its content. You need to secure the rights to high-quality content that will keep your subscribers coming back for more. This could include popular movies, TV shows, exclusive content, or even live sports events. Original programming is also a significant driver for success, as it creates unique value for your service that can't be found elsewhere.

Your streaming service should be easy to use and visually appealing. Users should be able to find the content they're looking for quickly and easily. A seamless user experience (UX) and user interface (UI) design are critical, as they impact how your audience perceives your service. The price of your streaming service is also important. You need to find a price point that is affordable for your target audience while allowing you to make a profit. Offering different pricing tiers, such as basic, premium, or family plans, can cater to different consumer preferences and encourage subscriptions.

Monetise your service through diverse revenue streams

There are several ways to monetise a streaming service, including:

  • Subscription fees: The most common method of monetising a streaming service. Users pay a monthly or annual fee to access the service.

  • Advertising: Another popular way to monetise a streaming service. Partner with advertisers to display their ads within the service, either as pre-roll, mid-roll, or post-roll ads.

  • Selling merchandise: Although less common, selling merchandise like t-shirts, hats, and mugs with your service's logo or branding can generate additional revenue.

  • Selling premium content: Offer exclusive content such as episodes, behind-the-scenes footage, or deleted scenes to your most dedicated fans for an additional cost.

  • Pay-per-view (PPV) events: If your streaming service offers live events, such as sports or concerts, offering them on a pay-per-view basis can be a lucrative revenue stream.

No matter how good your service is, there will always be customers who need help. Ensure you have a team of customer service representatives available to assist customers with any problems they may have. Offer support through various channels, such as live chat, email, and social media, to accommodate different customer preferences.

Optimise technical infrastructure for global distribution

In addition to the factors listed above, you also need to consider a number of technical considerations when launching a streaming service, such as:

  • Content delivery network (CDN): A CDN is a network of servers distributed around the world, enabling the quick and reliable delivery of content to users, regardless of their location.

  • Content protection: Protect your content from piracy using methods like DRM (digital rights management) and watermarking.

  • Latency: Minimise latency to ensure a seamless streaming experience for users. Optimise buffering algorithms, and use adaptive bitrate streaming to accommodate varying network conditions.

  • Bandwidth: Ensure adequate bandwidth to support the number of users expected to be streaming simultaneously.

  • Security: Protect user data from unauthorised access, theft, or modification. Implement robust security measures, such as encryption and secure authentication, to maintain the integrity of your streaming service.
